# Product Requirements & Brand Guidelines

## Business Context
**MoodOverMuscle** - Postnatal fitness platform for mothers on Queensland's Sunshine Coast. Prioritizes mental wellbeing alongside physical fitness through personalized training and community building.

## Core Value Proposition
- **FREE first session** (no payment/commitment) - ALWAYS emphasize this
- Safe postnatal fitness recovery with certified trainer Emily
- "M.O.M.unity" community building over competition
- Flexible training options (1-on-1, small groups, mums & bubs)

## Content & Messaging Rules

### Required Language Patterns
- **Conversational**: "Hi mama", "lovely", "we get it"
- **Supportive**: Acknowledge challenges without patronizing
- **Inclusive**: All fitness levels, life circumstances welcome
- **Community-focused**: "M.O.M.unity", "stronger together"

### Forbidden Language
- "Bounce back" or "pre-baby body" terminology
- "Six-pack pressure" or unrealistic expectations
- Intimidating fitness jargon
- Competitive or comparison-based messaging

### Key Messaging Hierarchy
1. **Primary**: "Mood Over Muscle" - mental health = physical health
2. **Safety**: Postnatal specialization, certified trainer
3. **Community**: Belonging over competition
4. **Accessibility**: FREE session, flexible options

## UI/UX Requirements

### FREE Session Emphasis
- Green color coding for FREE elements (`from-green-500 to-emerald-500`)
- Visual prominence with badges, icons, animations
- Repeated across hero, navigation, forms, CTAs
- Risk reversal language: "No payment", "No commitment", "No credit card"

### Trust Building Elements
- Emily's certifications prominently displayed
- Personal photos (family, action shots)
- Client testimonials with first name + last initial format
- Physical address and contact transparency
- Gallery showing real community members

### Form Design Principles
- Multi-step approach to reduce overwhelm
- Progress indicators and completion feedback
- Personal questions about goals/preferences
- Constant FREE session reinforcement
- Mobile-first, touch-friendly design
